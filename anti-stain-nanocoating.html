Certified by Sasmira Institute for up to 60 washes (Tested on Cotton)

CHALLENGES

Staining

Fabrics, textiles, and leather are prone to staining due to spills, dirt, oils, and various contaminants encountered in industrial settings. Stains can be difficult to remove and may impact the appearance, functionality, and lifespan of these materials.

Maintenance Efforts

Cleaning and maintaining fabrics, textiles, and leather in industrial environments can be time-consuming and labor-intensive. Without proper protection, these materials require frequent cleaning and may be susceptible to damage or discoloration.

UV Degradation

Prolonged exposure to UV rays can cause fabrics, textiles, and leather to fade, lose color vibrancy, and weaken in structure. This poses a challenge, especially in industries where these materials are exposed to sunlight or artificial UV sources.

Stain Retention

Absorbent nature of fabrics, textiles, and some types of leather can lead to deep penetration and retention of stains, making them challenging to remove completely. This can affect the aesthetics and functionality of the materials.

Durability and Longevity

Fabrics, textiles, and leather used in industrial applications need to withstand rigorous conditions and maintain their durability over time. Factors like staining, UV exposure, and wear can compromise their longevity and performance.

Perspiration

Perspiration from skin can cause stains on fabric and leather surfaces, especially in areas that are in close contact with skin, such as clothing, upholstery, and car seats.
